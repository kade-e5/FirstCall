Durango Property Restoration and Water Damage Services +

Water damage restoration services, such as First Call, conduct water mitigation to maintain your property in top condition. This multi-step process includes removing and pumping out water from flooded areas. The water extraction phase occurs prior to cleaning and drying, utilizing dehumidifiers and powerful air movers to ensure thorough drying.

Following a detailed assessment of your property, PuroClean technicians will identify the necessary restoration steps. We employ thermal imaging and moisture meters to locate moisture on and within various surfaces and structures. Additionally, we use EPA-registered disinfectants to clean, sanitize, and deodorize affected areas. Our efforts focus on eliminating mold-related concerns and deodorizing walls, floors, crawl spaces, and wall cavities.

Vanities and cabinets

Compared to drywall, which dries more quickly than other structural components, our aim is to minimize damage as much as possible. Cabinets, however, can pose challenges based on the extent of water exposure and the materials used in their construction. Thankfully, Mold Medic's skilled water damage restoration experts utilize advanced drying methods to speed up evaporation, striving to dry most materials efficiently and restore them to their pre-loss condition in terms of appearance, cleanliness, and dryness.

Sewage backup cleanup

In cases of sewage backup or flooding, you can rely on First Call experts to remove harmful pathogens that pose serious health risks. Sewage emergencies create challenging conditions for both residential and commercial property owners. Engaging certified water remediation professionals is crucial for effectively addressing a sewage flood. Our specialists apply EPA-registered disinfectants to affected areas and employ specialized cleaning techniques, which may involve removing certain materials that cannot be salvaged, following industry standards.

Wood flooring

Moisture removal from wood flooring demands a highly controlled restoration approach. Specialized equipment—such as heat sources, desiccant-based dehumidifiers, and custom drying panels—is often used to target deep-set moisture. Although this method typically preserves the floor's finish, certain areas may still require refinishing to bring back the wood’s original condition.

Crawl spaces and wall cavities

Experienced restoration professionals are equipped to address the hidden dangers of moisture trapped within walls, ceilings, floors, and crawl spaces. When ignored, these areas can deteriorate, leading to structural damage and mold growth. Our team prioritizes identifying and thoroughly treating all water-damaged zones to stop the spread of mold, microbes, and further structural issues before they escalate.
